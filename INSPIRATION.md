# Design & research inspiration

Reference links for Umai's direction: what similar apps already do, what a "cute"
visual identity can look like, and where to find mascot ideas. All links verified
live as of 2026-07-24 (one dead one below is called out on purpose, as a cautionary
data point, not a mistake).

---

## 1. Vegan / vegetarian restaurant finders

- **[HappyCow](https://www.happycow.net/)** — the long-standing category leader:
  240,000+ listings across 185 countries, reviews, photos, diet-type filtering. The
  closest direct comparison for Umai's core feature set.
- **[HappyCow — Tokyo](https://www.happycow.net/asia/japan/tokyo/)** — the same
  product scoped to exactly Umai's city; useful to compare listing density, category
  filters, and how they handle Japanese-language venue info.
- **[Vegman](https://www.vegman.org/)** — a smaller, community-driven vegetarian/vegan
  map and directory (companion to a mobile app) — a good contrast to HappyCow's scale.
- **[VegNews — vegan-finding apps & tools roundup](https://vegnews.com/vegan-businesses-near-you-apps-tools)**
  — a curated comparison of several finder apps in one place, useful for quick
  competitive scanning.

**Cautionary note:** [Abillion](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Abillion), a
well-regarded HappyCow alternative with a reviews + charity-donation angle,
**shut down in March 2026** after failing to raise further funding despite reaching
3.1M active users — worth knowing as a reminder that scale alone doesn't guarantee
survival in this niche.

## 2. Cute websites (green / vegan / crayon-cute aesthetics)

Curated galleries — best for browsing many live examples at once:

- **[Awwwards — hand-drawn elements showcase](https://www.awwwards.com/showcase-of-websites-using-hand-drawn-elements.html)**
  — award-recognized sites using sketchy/hand-drawn design.
- **[99designs — hand-drawn website inspiration](https://99designs.com/inspiration/websites/hand-drawn)**
  — another curated hand-drawn/crayon-adjacent gallery.
- **[Qode Interactive — beautiful pastel websites](https://qodeinteractive.com/magazine/beautiful-pastel-websites/)**
  — soft pastel palette examples, several skew green/natural.
- **[Muffin Group — websites with a green color palette](https://muffingroup.com/blog/green-color-palette/)**
  — real sites built around green, relevant to Umai's leaf palette specifically.
- **[Land-book](https://land-book.com/)** and **[Lapa Ninja](https://www.lapa.ninja/)**
  — general, searchable/filterable design galleries (thousands of live sites) —
  good for hunting down more "cute" examples beyond any single curated list.

Specific real, well-known sites worth studying directly:

- **[Oatly](https://www.oatly.com/)** — hand-drawn typography, playful conversational
  copy, "anti-polished" personality; the clearest real-world example of a food brand
  leaning fully into hand-drawn/crayon charm.
- **[Duolingo](https://www.duolingo.com/)** — rounded shapes, bright green, a mascot
  woven through the entire product rather than just the logo.

## 3. Mascot design ideas

- **[Duolingo Brand Guidelines](https://design.duolingo.com/)** — Duolingo's own
  public brand site, showing exactly how a mascot's rules, poses, illustration style,
  and tone of voice get documented — a genuinely useful structural reference, not
  just pretty pictures.
- **[Character Design References](https://characterdesignreferences.com/)** — a huge,
  long-running library of character/mascot art, model sheets, and tutorials from
  professional illustrators.
- **[Dribbble — Mascot tag](https://dribbble.com/tags/mascot)** and
  **[Dribbble — Mascot Logo tag](https://dribbble.com/tags/mascot-logo)** — large,
  browsable feeds of mascot concepts and finished mascot logos.
- **[Behance — "mascot design" search](https://www.behance.net/search/projects/mascot%20design)**
  — full case studies (not just single images) showing a mascot's full design process.
- **[Raw.Studio — How Mascots Improve User Experience](https://raw.studio/blog/how-mascots-improve-user-experience/)**
  — a practical article on *why* and *where* a mascot helps a product, not just how
  to draw one — useful before picking more poses/moods for Umai's own mascot.
